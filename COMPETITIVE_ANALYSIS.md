# SP OMP Platform — Competitive Landscape Analysis
Prepared by: NDAI (ndai.pro) for SandPro LLC
Date: March 19, 2026

## Executive Summary
SandPro is building the **SP OMP (Objective Management Platform)** — a focused execution-tracking tool tailored to their industrial workflow. The two platforms evaluated as reference points were **Ninety.io** and **Bloom Growth** (formerly Traction Tools). Both are EOS-centric platforms designed for companies running the Entrepreneurial Operating System. Neither is built for industrial/oilfield operations. SP OMP has the opportunity to be purpose-built for SandPro's culture, workflow, and reporting needs — something neither competitor offers.

---

## Competitor 1: Ninety.io
**Overview:** Ninety is the market leader for EOS-based business operating software. Founded in 2017, it serves 38,000+ companies and 495,000+ users. It digitizes the full EOS toolkit: Vision/Traction Organizer, Accountability Charts, Scorecards, Rocks, To-Dos, Issues, and Level 10 Meetings.

**Pricing:**
* Free tier available
* Essentials: $8.40/user/month
* Accelerate: $9.80/user/month
* Thrive: $11.20/user/month (full features + AI)

**Strengths:**
* Mature, polished product with large user base
* Full EOS framework integration
* Strong scorecard and meeting management tools
* Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 integrations

**Weaknesses:**
* Deeply tied to EOS methodology — poor fit for non-EOS companies
* Mobile app is underdeveloped and buggy
* No API access for external integrations
* Scorecard administration is difficult
* Requires all employees to be paid users to properly assign scorecards/rocks
* No Power BI integration or SQL-level data access
* Not designed for industrial/operations companies

---

## Competitor 2: Bloom Growth
**Overview:** Bloom Growth is a business growth platform combining operating system software, coaching, and methodology. It serves approximately 8,800 organizations. Originally built as the default tool for EOS implementation teams.

**Pricing:**
* Entry point: $249/month flat rate (not per-user)
* Annual commitment required ($2,988/year minimum)

**Strengths:**
* Flat-rate pricing (good for larger teams)
* Built-in coaching ecosystem
* Strong meeting management with automated minutes
* Works with multiple frameworks (EOS, Scaling Up, Pinnacle)

**Weaknesses:**
* Interface widely described as "outdated" and "clunky" in 2026 reviews
* Steep learning curve — not plug-and-play
* Poor mobile experience (laggy, limited)
* Expensive annual contracts with high switching costs
* No Power BI or direct data export capabilities

## SP OMP: Competitive Positioning
**What SP OMP Offers That Neither Competitor Does:**
* **Purpose-built** for SandPro's industrial operations workflow.
* **Power BI-ready** data model with SQL access and CSV export.
* **Clean accountability trail** (who assigned, who owns, what's overdue).
* **Metric tracking** with baseline/target/check-in model for improvement objectives.
* **Mobile-responsive** design for field and office use.
* **SandPro-branded UI** (orange/gunmetal industrial aesthetic).
* **No per-user SaaS fees** — owned by SandPro.

## Strategic Takeaway
SandPro doesn't need another generic EOS platform. The competitors are built for consulting-driven, methodology-heavy companies. SP OMP fills a gap: a lean, accountability-focused execution tool that integrates with SandPro's existing Power BI reporting and fits the way industrial teams actually work. The "build vs buy" math is clear — neither Ninety nor Bloom gives SandPro what they need, and both would require workarounds and per-seat licenses that don't fit.
